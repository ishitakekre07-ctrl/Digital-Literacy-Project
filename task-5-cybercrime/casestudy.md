Case Study: Identity Theft Through Data Breach 

Identity theft is a cybercrime in which a person’s personal information is stolen and used without their permission, usually for financial fraud or impersonation. This type of crime has become increasingly common with the growth of online platforms and digital data storage.

In this scenario, a college student named Riya signs up on a shopping website using her personal details, including her email, phone number, and saved card information. Step 1: The website later suffers a data breach due to weak security systems. Step 2: Hackers gain access to the database and steal users’ personal and financial information. Step 3: The stolen data is sold on the dark web. Step 4: A cybercriminal purchases Riya’s information and uses it to create fake accounts and make unauthorized transactions. Step 5: Riya begins receiving bank alerts for transactions she never made and realizes her identity has been misused.

Young adults, online shoppers, and people who reuse passwords across platforms are usually the primary targets of identity theft. Lack of awareness and poor cybersecurity habits increase their vulnerability.

The consequences of identity theft can be serious, including financial loss, damage to credit score, and emotional distress. Victims may also face difficulty proving their identity and restoring their accounts. In severe cases, legal complications may arise if the stolen identity is used for illegal activities. This highlights the importance of strong passwords, secure websites, and awareness of online safety practices.
